HNS FAQ

What is HNS?

HNS (short for Handshake) is the name of a cryptocurrency coin and blockchain.  With it, you can own decentralized domain names on the internet.

The Handshake blockchain is the primary record-keeping core of the HNS Domain Name System registry.  If you want to go to a website with a handshake name, the blockchain is where your browser will get the directions.

It is secured by proof-of-work mining, with machines and nodes located everywhere on Earth.  Unlike many other machine miners, these are drastically more efficient and can be run on regular outlet power.  Anyone can run one.

These miners “hash” (decode & process) all of the transactions for domain names.  And they are responsible for minting the actual coins (HNS) into being too.  Minting will continue to happen until the total scheduled supply is out in the world, and then miners will only handle blockchain data transactions.

In order to register, transfer, auction or update a domain name, you must engage with the blockchain.  Wallets are used for this.  They hold HNS coins, and also your domain names.  There is no required 3rd party involvement if you want to own a domain name.  You may buy and hold them in your own wallet. 

Why should I use a Handshake domain name?

1.  Once you buy it, you own it forever.  It is an asset which you can use, sell or lease.

2.  No one can cancel you, turn you off or censor your domain name because of your beliefs, opinions, religious or political views.  In current times, these things are happening more often, so decentralized domains are a good way to secure and protect free speech.

3.  Saves you money.  No more paying for expensive yearly renewals to rent a name you can never own or negotiate a purchase on.  Handshake names do require a blockchain ping update every 2 years, but that is it.  This update costs 2 HNS, so it is a good idea to keep HNS coins in your domain wallet to cover this.  If you buy a name now (Aug 2022), you can buy and hold enough HNS to cover 50 years of registration for $3.00 !

Is this the same as ENS?

No.  ENS is a similar concept, but it uses the Ethereum blockchain for the transactions instead of it’s own miners.

Decentralized blockchain domains are not a new idea.  A similar technology was tried in the past called Namecoin, which allowed you to register a “.bit” name with a blockchain record.  It is still operational, but it was difficult to use and not as logical as things are now.  There will likely be a continued move towards these domains as time goes on. 

The most interesting thing about decentralized names is they can be used for much more than just a website. They can function as wallets, ids, sms, secure logins, voip addresses – the list goes on. You can even “tokenize” HNS domain names.  So if you like using Ethereum, you can turn your Handshake name into an NFT contract there and use it in a totally different way.  Technology is pretty cool.

Handshake names are very secure and sovereign.  The blockchain itself originates from a fork of Bitcoin.  The assets are easy to hold in a wallet and it doesn’t have high transaction fees like Ethereum tends to have.  For many, Handshake is a better option.

How can I get an HNS domain name?

Depending on how you want to use it, there are multiple ways to acquire Handshake names

1.  Search for names in the Bob Wallet or at Namebase.  If your name is available you will be able to start an auction for it.  The process will take about 15 days.  If the name is already owned and for sale at Namebase, you will also have the option to buy it or make an offer there.

2.  Discover names to buy through private sellers, aftermarket name brokers and listing sites.  There are some links to these things in the LINKS section here.  This is good if you are flexible and don’t have a permanent name selected yet.

3.  Use a third-party name registrar in the traditional web realm.  You will most likely have to pay an expensive yearly renewal fee, just like the old ways.  The only quasi-benefit is you do not have to be responsible for the wallet and domain name.  But you still can be censored and obstructed, and you do not own the name.  Generally it is not worth it.
